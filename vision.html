The vision

Maple Works' vision is reflected in its logo which is made up of three cogs enveloped by a geo tag pin. The cogs represent three types of business users - freelancers, startups and established companies. The geo tag pin represents the local community and its importance in sustaining these businesses.

Maple Works is envisioned to be a unique coworking space in the outer London area. We plan to bring the businesses together with the local community with coworking, networking and mentoring as a foundation of a sustainable and mutually beneficial relationship.

Founders

David Randall

Cofounder
David has been in consultancy since 1999, initially in B2B and latterly B2C and has founded or co-founded 5 businesses including Bering Internet Ltd, the original, award-winning startup in the Kingston Innovation Centre in 2003. Latterly David has focussed on working with individuals. In 2010 David created ‘Workoco’ to help people to settle their career dilemmas allowing them to start a business with confidence. Workoco is offered at Maple Works. He also co-founded Vital Kingston to promote ‘third places’, places that bring the community together.

Richard Sealtiel

Cofounder
Richard’s passion is for turning businesses that haven’t reached their potential - unpolished jewels - into local landmarks. The Surbiton Flyer pub which sits next door to Surbiton’s iconic 1930’s station is one of his creations. The Gordon Bennett! pub next door to Maple Works is another. Richard is also co-founder of the Surbiton Business Community and the Surbiton Farmers Market, two more cornerstones of Surbiton’s vibrant community.
